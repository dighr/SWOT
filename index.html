Waterborne diseases are among the leading threats facing people in refugee and IDP camps. Ensuring adequate quantities of safe water is essential for protecting public health.

However, the emergency water treatment guidelines currently used by humanitarian agencies are not based on any field evidence. This means that they often fail to ensure that water remains safe where people actually drink it.

The Safe Water Optimization Tool fills this critical public health gap. The SWOT will soon be live to use online; in the meanwhile, explore this site to learn more about the SWOT.
