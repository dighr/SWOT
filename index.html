Waterborne diseases are among the leading threats facing people in refugee and internally displaced persons (IDP) camps. Ensuring adequate quantities of safe water is essential for protecting public health.
But current guidelines for water treatmnet in humanitarian emergencies are not based on field evidence and often fail to ensure that water remains safe to drink at the point of consumption.
The Safe Water Optimization Tool is here to fill this critical public health gap. Explore this site to learn more.
