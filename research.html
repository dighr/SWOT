Research

The SWOT is built on years of water safety research conducted in refugee camps around the world. Here are the key papers that underlie the SWOT.

Effectiveness of emergency water treatment practices in refugee camps in South Sudan.

Ali, S. I., Ali, S. S., & Fesselet, J.  (2015).
Bulletin of the World Health Organization, 93(8), 550–558.

Evidence-based chlorination targets for household watey safety in humanitarian settings: Recommendations from a multi-site study in refugee camps in South Sudan, Jordan, and Rwanda

Ali, S. I., Ali, S. S., & Fesselet, J.  (2021).
Water Research, 189(116642)

Forecasting point-of-consumption chlorine residual in refugee settlements using ensembles of artificial neural networks

De Santi,M., Khan, U.T., Arnold, M., Fesselet, J.-F., & Ali, S.I.  (2021).
NPJ Clean Water 4, 35.

The Safe Water Optimization Tool Artificial Neural Network Analytics for the SWOT v2.0

De Santi,M., & S. I. Ali  (2021).
White Paper. Dahdaleh Institute for Global Health Research: Toronto, Canada.
